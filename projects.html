Rössing North: Exploration



  • Adjacent to Rio Tinto’s Rossing Mine
  • Proven Uranium Mineralisation (up to 400ppm)
  • Similar Geology to Rossing Mine (Active for 35 years)
  • Excellent Infrastructure on License Area
  • Mining Friendly Jurisdiction

Rössing North is located directly adjacent to Rio Tinto’s Rössing Mine and with excellent existing infrastructure. Cobra have identified several anomalous zones of mineralisation analogous with the regional deposits such as Rossing, Husab and Trekkopje.

Resource Estimate

Both primary Alaskite and Secondary Calcrete mineralisation are present on site. With the Alsakite geology typical of Rossing, Rossing South, Valencia and Etango in the Damara Orogenic Belt.

JORC 2012 Resource Estimate, Novemeber 2015
Inferred 15.62Mt 266ppm 9.14 Mlbs U3O8


Cobra drilled in excess of 3,700m across Areas 1 & 3 with every hole intercepting mineralisation which has resulted in a JORC 2012 Maiden Resource of 9.14Mlbs U3O8 completed by SRK Cardiff.

With a 100% drilling target success rate no money was wasted during the delineation of the maiden resource.



Both Areas 1 & 3 remain open along strike and at depth with current drilling testing to a maximum depth of only 100m.

An independent SRK report estimates that a potential 180Mlbs U3O8 could be present at Rössing North, both as Alaskite and Calcrete hosted mineralisation covering 11 target areas.

Further potential for Base Metals exists on site while critical metals such as Vanadium are present and as of yet untested.
